<p><p>The food-grade phosphate market consists of several key types, including Sodium tripolyphosphate (STPP), Sodium hexametaphosphate (SHMP), Sodium acid pyrophosphate (SAPP), and Trisodium phosphate (TSPP). STPP serves as a preservative and emulsifier in processed foods, while SHMP is used as a thickening agent and sequestrant. SAPP functions as a leavening agent in baked goods, whereas TSPP acts as a buffer and acidity regulator. "Other" includes various niche phosphates catering to specific food applications, enhancing quality and shelf-life.</p></p>
