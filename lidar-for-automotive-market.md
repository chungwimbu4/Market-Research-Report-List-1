<p><ul><li>Mechanical LiDAR</li><li>Solid State LiDAR</li></ul></p>
<p><p>LiDAR for the automotive market is classified into two main types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid-State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR utilizes rotating components to capture 3D environmental data, offering high precision and long-range capabilities. However, this complexity can lead to higher costs and vulnerability to wear. In contrast, Solid-State LiDAR employs fixed elements and is more compact and robust, making it suitable for mass production. It typically has lower costs and improved reliability, driving its adoption in advanced driver-assistance systems and autonomous vehicles.</p></p>
