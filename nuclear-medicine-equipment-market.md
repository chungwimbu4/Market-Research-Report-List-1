<p><p>Nuclear medicine equipment consists of various imaging technologies, primarily categorized into hybrid and standalone systems. Hybrid Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) combines traditional SPECT with other imaging modalities, such as computed tomography (CT), enhancing diagnostic capabilities through anatomical and functional imaging integration. In contrast, standalone SPECT systems operate independently, focusing solely on functional imaging of organs using gamma radiation. Both market types are essential for determining disease presence, guiding treatment decisions, and improving patient outcomes in nuclear medicine.</p></p>
