<p><p>The Endocrine Disruption Screening market encompasses two primary types: in vitro screening and in vivo screening. In vitro screening involves laboratory tests using cell cultures or tissues to assess the effects of chemicals on hormonal activity, providing a faster and cost-effective approach. In contrast, in vivo screening involves testing on whole organisms, allowing for a comprehensive evaluation of the biological impact and potential risks of endocrine disruptors in a living system. Both methods are crucial for regulatory compliance and ensuring public health safety.</p></p>
