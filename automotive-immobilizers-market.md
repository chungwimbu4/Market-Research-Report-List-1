<p><p>The automotive immobilizers market is segmented into two main types: OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) and aftermarket. OEM immobilizers are integrated into vehicles during manufacturing, providing factory-level security and compatibility with the vehicle's systems. In contrast, aftermarket immobilizers are purchased and installed by vehicle owners or third-party providers, often offering enhanced features and compatibility with various models. Both types aim to prevent unauthorized vehicle access and theft, but they cater to different consumer needs and preferences.</p></p>
