<p><ul><li>Phase Change Refrigeration</li><li>Joule-Thomson Throttling Refrigeration</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cryosurgical equipment for cancer treatment utilizes advanced refrigeration methods to achieve the extreme cold temperatures required for effective tumor ablation. The Phase Change Refrigeration type employs materials that change state, absorbing heat during the transition, thus cooling the surgical instrument. In contrast, Joule-Thomson Throttling Refrigeration relies on the expansion of compressed gas, resulting in temperature drop, to achieve cryogenic conditions. Both technologies are essential for precise and controlled tissue destruction, enhancing treatment outcomes in cryosurgery.</p></p>
