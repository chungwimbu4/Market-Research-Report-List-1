<p><p>Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES) involves storing energy by compressing air in underground caverns or containers. Traditional CAES utilizes adiabatic or diabatic methods, where compressed air is released to drive turbines for electricity generation. Liquid Gas CAES employs cryogenic technology to liquefy air, storing it at low temperatures for energy release upon warming. Other markets may include hybrid systems combining CAES with renewable sources, or novel methods like pneumatic energy storage, expanding the application of compressed air technologies in energy management.</p></p>
