<p><p>The medical asparaginase market comprises three primary types based on their sources: Escherichia coli-derived asparaginase, Erwinia chrysanthemi-derived asparaginase, and pegylated asparaginase. Escherichia coli asparaginase is widely used due to its efficacy in treating acute lymphoblastic leukemia but can induce allergic reactions. Erwinia chrysanthemi asparaginase serves as an alternative for patients with hypersensitivity to E. coli variants. Pegylated asparaginase is modified to enhance stability and reduce immunogenicity, offering prolonged circulation time and improved therapeutic outcomes.</p></p>
