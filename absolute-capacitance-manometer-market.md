<p><p>Absolute capacitance manometers operate in two primary market types: heated and unheated. Heated manometers are designed to minimize measurement errors caused by temperature variations, making them suitable for high-temperature applications or environments with fluctuating conditions. Unheated manometers, on the other hand, rely on the ambient temperature for their operation, typically used in stable conditions where temperature changes are minimal. Each type serves distinct applications based on the required precision, environmental challenges, and operational efficiency.</p></p>
